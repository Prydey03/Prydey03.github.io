Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) Education

What is BAC?

Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) is a measure of the concentration of alcohol in your bloodstream. It's a crucial factor in determining your level of impairment and the legal limits for driving. Understanding your BAC can help prevent accidents and legal issues associated with alcohol consumption.

Using the BAC Calculator

Our BAC calculator uses a formula based on scientific research to estimate your BAC. It considers factors such as sex, weight, quantity of alcohol, and time since your last drink. While it provides a close estimate, please note that actual BAC may vary based on individual factors. Be aware of your local authority's legal BAC limit before driving.

Responsible Drinking Tips

Responsible drinking is essential to ensure your safety and the safety of others. Here are some tips for responsible drinking:

  • Know your limits: Understand how alcohol affects you personally and drink within those limits.
  • Pace yourself: Consume alcohol slowly and alternate with non-alcoholic beverages.
  • Plan ahead: Arrange for a designated driver or alternative transportation if you plan to drink. Don't drink and drive.

Understanding Legal Limits (NSW)

In New South Wales (NSW), it's important to be aware of the legal limits regarding BAC. The legal BAC limit for full license holders in NSW is 0.05. Learner and provisional license holders must maintain a zero BAC while driving. Breaking these limits can result in severe penalties and legal consequences.
